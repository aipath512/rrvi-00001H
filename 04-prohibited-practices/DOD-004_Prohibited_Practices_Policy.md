# 1. PURPOSE

This policy defines the organization's mandatory rules for preventing the development, deployment, procurement, or use of Artificial Intelligence systems that fall under prohibited practices as defined by Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (EU AI Act).

The objective is to ensure that no prohibited AI system is intentionally or unintentionally introduced into organizational operations.

---

# 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:

- All employees and contractors  
- All business units and subsidiaries  
- All third-party vendors and service providers  
- All AI systems, tools, and services used directly or indirectly  
- All data processing workflows involving AI systems  

---

# 3. REGULATORY BASIS

This policy is based on Article 5 of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (EU AI Act), which defines prohibited AI practices.

---

# 4. POLICY STATEMENT

The organization SHALL NOT:

- Develop prohibited AI systems  
- Deploy prohibited AI systems  
- Integrate prohibited AI systems into internal workflows  
- Procure prohibited AI systems from third parties  
- Intentionally facilitate prohibited AI use  

Any suspected prohibited AI usage MUST be immediately escalated for compliance review.

---

# 5. PROHIBITED PRACTICES (CONTROLLED LIST)

The following categories are considered prohibited under applicable regulation:

- Manipulative or deceptive AI systems causing significant harm  
- Exploitation of vulnerabilities of specific groups  
- Social scoring systems in prohibited contexts  
- Real-time remote biometric identification in restricted public spaces (subject to legal exceptions)  
- Any AI system explicitly defined as prohibited under Article 5 updates  

---

# 6. CONTROL REQUIREMENTS

The organization SHALL implement:

- Pre-deployment AI assessment  
- Vendor due diligence for AI tools  
- AI system inventory maintenance  
- Periodic compliance review  
- Incident reporting mechanism  

---

# 7. RESPONSIBILITIES

## 7.1 Management

- Ensure enforcement of this policy  
- Provide necessary resources  
- Approve exceptions (if legally permitted)

## 7.2 Compliance Officer

- Maintain prohibited AI register  
- Conduct periodic audits  
- Validate AI system classification  
- Maintain evidence records  

## 7.3 AI System Owners

- Declare AI usage  
- Provide system documentation  
- Cooperate with compliance assessments  

---

# 8. PROHIBITED AI ASSESSMENT PROCESS

Before any AI system is used, the following MUST be performed:

1. Identify AI system functionality  
2. Map against prohibited categories  
3. Evaluate intended use case  
4. Assess downstream risk  
5. Document findings in compliance register  
6. Approve or reject usage  

---

# 9. EXCEPTION POLICY

No exception shall permit the use of an explicitly prohibited AI practice.

Where legal interpretation is uncertain, the system SHALL be escalated to compliance/legal review prior to deployment.

---

# 10. EVIDENCE REQUIREMENTS

The following records MUST be maintained:

- AI system inventory entries  
- Prohibited practices checklist  
- Risk assessment documentation  
- Compliance declaration  
- Vendor documentation (if applicable)  
- Audit logs  

---

# 11. MONITORING & REVIEW

This policy shall be:

- Reviewed annually  
- Updated upon regulatory changes  
- Audited during internal compliance cycles  

---

# 12. NON-COMPLIANCE

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

- Suspension of AI system usage  
- Disciplinary action  
- Contract termination (for vendors)  
- Regulatory reporting where applicable
