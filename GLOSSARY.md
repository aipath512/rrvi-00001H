# GLOSSARY

## EU AI Act Compliance Management System™

Repository: RRVI™

Version: 1.0.2

---

# Purpose

This glossary defines the terminology used throughout the EU AI Act Compliance Management System.

---

# Terms

## AI System

A machine-based system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy and capable of generating outputs such as predictions, recommendations, decisions or content.

---

## AI Governance

The framework of policies, procedures, responsibilities and controls used to manage AI systems.

---

## AI Governance Owner

The individual responsible for coordinating the organization's AI Compliance Management System.

---

## AI System Owner

The individual responsible for a specific AI system throughout its lifecycle.

---

## Audit

A systematic, independent and documented process for evaluating compliance.

---

## Checklist

A controlled document used to verify completion of required activities.

---

## Compliance

The state of conforming with applicable legal, regulatory and organizational requirements.

---

## Controlled Document

A document that is uniquely identified, versioned, approved and managed under document control.

---

## Declaration

Management's formal statement confirming implementation of a compliance activity.

---

## Evidence

Objective information demonstrating that an activity has been performed.

---

## High-Risk AI System

An AI system classified as High-Risk under Regulation (EU) 2024/1689.

---

## Human Oversight

Measures ensuring that natural persons supervise AI system operation where required.

---

## Incident

An event affecting the availability, integrity, confidentiality, compliance or safe operation of an AI system.

---

## Inspection

A formal review performed by a regulatory authority or authorized body.

---

## Policy

A high-level statement establishing management intentions and direction.

---

## Procedure

A documented sequence of activities describing how a process is performed.

---

## Register

A controlled record containing operational compliance information.

---

## Risk Classification

The process of determining the applicable EU AI Act risk category for an AI system.

---

## Traceability

The ability to follow relationships between policies, procedures, records, evidence and declarations.

---

## Version Control

The systematic management of document revisions throughout their lifecycle.
